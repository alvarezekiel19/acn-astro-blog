Stand at the base of an exposed rock face and you are, technically, looking directly at deep time. Each band is a few thousand to a few million years, compressed into something you can touch.

This essay is an attempt to borrow geology's sense of scale for everyday anxieties — the ones that feel enormous on a Tuesday and invisible from a few hundred million years out.
